Calibration positioning:

This section will explain and show how to position the AR Drone 2.0 during calibration with the Calibrating_The_AR_Drone_Sensors GUI.

Accelerometer X+ direction positioning:

Place the drone such that the camera is pointing straight up and does not move. An easy way to do this is to let the back of the AR Drone rest on a flat surface while pressing the feet of the AR Drone against a flat vertical wall and is shown on the image below.

Accelerometer X- direction positioning:

Place the drone such that the camera is pointing straight down and does not move. An easy way to do this is to let the front of the AR Drone rest on a flat surface while pressing the feet of the AR Drone against a flat vertical wall and is shown on the image below.

Accelerometer Y+ direction positioning:

Place the drone on its side with the top facing you and the camera facing right. An easy way to do this is to let the side of the AR Drone rest on a flat surface while pressing the feet of the AR Drone against a flat vertical wall and is shown on the image below.

Accelerometer Y- direction positioning:

Place the drone on its side with the top facing you and the camera facing left. An easy way to do this is to let the side of the AR Drone rest on a flat surface while pressing the feet of the AR Drone against a flat vertical wall and is shown on the image below.

Accelerometer Z+ direction positioning:

Place the AR Drone 2.0 on a flat horizontal surface such that it is standing on its feet and does not move such as shown on the image below.

Accelerometer Z- direction positioning:

Place the AR Drone 2.0 such that it is perfectly level and upside down (its feet should point at the sky) and does not move. An easy way to achieve this is to press the AR Drone against the bottom of a level table. The orientation the drone should be in is shown below.

Place the AR Drone 2.0 on a flat horizontal surface such that it is standing on its feet and does not move such as shown on the image below.

Magnetometer calibration movement:

To calibrate the magnetometer the AR Drone 2.0 has to be rotated in a pattern. It should provide maximal and minimal exposure of all three components of the magnetometer to theearth magnetic field. To do this it is recommend to perform multiple rotations of the AR Drone 2.0 around its pitch, roll and yaw axis.
